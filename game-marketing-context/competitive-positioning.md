### Common Forces for Our Portfolio

#### Universal PUSH Factors (What drives players away from alternatives)
- "App store games are too big (500MB+ for a puzzle?)"
- "I'm tired of games that need internet + account + permissions"
- "Mobile games feel like ad machines, not games"
- "Same gameplay recycled with different skins"
- "Pay-to-win killed the fun"

#### Universal PULL Factors (What draws them to us)
- "One tap and I'm playing â€” insanely fast"
- "No download, no account, no permissions"
- "Actually fun, not just a Skinner box"
- "Fresh indie games, not corporate clones"
- "Play right in Telegram without switching apps"

#### Universal HABIT Factors (What keeps them elsewhere)
- "I've been playing Candy Crush for years, my progress..."
- "My friends play other games"
- "I already have games on my phone"
- "I know how mobile games work"

#### Universal ANXIETY Factors (What scares them about trying)
- "Browser games look cheap / are they safe?"
- "Will it work on my phone?"
- "Is it going to crash or lag?"
- "Will my data be safe in a Telegram game?"

## Framework 4: Positioning Statement Generator

```
For [target persona]
who [situation/need],
[Game Name] is a [micro-category]

[Game Name] [key differentiator].
```

```
that deliver fun in one tap.
```

## Framework 6: Competitive Response Playbook

### When a Competitor Launches Something Similar

```
1. ASSESS (1 day)
   - How similar is it really?
   - What's their reach/distribution?
   - What do they do better? Worse?

2. DIFFERENTIATE (1 week)
   - Double down on our unique hook
   - Ship the feature they don't have
   - Collect "why we're different" player quotes

3. COMMUNICATE (ongoing)
   - Update positioning if needed
   - Don't mention competitor by name
   - Focus on "why us" not "why not them"
```

### When We Enter a Crowded Genre

```
1. Find the "missing" quadrant in the competitive map
2. Build ONE feature that no competitor has
3. Position around that ONE thing
4. Let the portfolio ("30+ games by the same dev") be social proof
```

## Quick Reference: Positioning Don'ts

| Don't | Why | Do Instead |
|-------|-----|-----------|
| "Best game ever" | Unbelievable, generic | "The fastest-loading puzzle game on Telegram" |
| "Like X but better" | Positions you as a copy | "A fresh take on [genre]" |
| "For everyone" | Positions you for no one | "Perfect for your commute" |
| "AAA quality" | Sets wrong expectations | "Polished indie craftsmanship" |
| "Revolutionary" | Eye-roll inducing | Show, don't tell (GIF > words) |
| Compare on features | Feature war = lose | Compare on experience |
