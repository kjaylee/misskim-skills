## Persona 2: ğŸ  Bedroom Indie Fan

### Demographics
- **Age:** 16-28
- **Location:** Global, English-speaking skew
- **Device:** Desktop/laptop (primary), phone (secondary)
- **Income:** Student, part-time work, allowance

### Behavior
- **Plays when:** Evening wind-down, weekends, procrastination sessions
- **Session length:** 15-60 minutes
- **Discovery:** itch.io browsing, Reddit (r/IndieGaming, r/WebGames), YouTube indie roundups, Steam discovery queue
- **Spending:** Will pay $2-10 for a game they love. Supports devs on principle.
- **Loyalty:** High for games they connect with â€” follows dev on social, joins Discord

### Motivations
- Discover unique, artistic, or experimental games
- Support independent creators
- Find "hidden gems" before they blow up
- Experience something different from AAA
- Creative inspiration (many are aspiring devs themselves)

### Frustrations
- Same AAA formulas recycled endlessly
- Indie games that are "just another platformer"
- Poor controls or UI on browser games
- No way to save progress across sessions
- Devs who abandon games after launch

### How to Reach Them
- **Channel:** itch.io, Reddit devlogs, indie game YouTube channels, game jams
- **Message:** "Handcrafted by a solo dev. Play free. No strings."
- **Hook:** Unique mechanic + dev personality + polished feel

### Verbatim Language
- "This has so much charm for a browser game"
- "Wishlisted! Love the art direction"
- "Solo dev? That's impressive"

## Persona 3: ğŸ“± Mobile Snacker

### Demographics
- **Age:** 25-45
- **Location:** Global, mobile-first markets (SEA, LATAM, India)
- **Device:** Android (80%), budget to mid-range
- **Income:** Working professional

### Behavior
- **Plays when:** Micro-moments â€” waiting for coffee, before sleep, bathroom break (yes, really)
- **Session length:** 1-5 minutes
- **Discovery:** App store search ("free puzzle game"), Google Play featured, friend recommendations
- **Spending:** Ad-tolerant if fair. Spends on remove-ads ($1.99-3.99) or cosmetics.
- **Loyalty:** Moderate â€” keeps 2-3 games in rotation, deletes the rest

### Motivations
- Quick dopamine hit
- Feel smart (puzzle solved!)
- Compete with friends or global leaderboard
- Background activity while watching TV

### Frustrations
- Games that drain battery
- "This app requires 500MB" for a simple puzzle
- Intrusive interstitial ads every 30 seconds
- Complicated mechanics that need tutorials

### How to Reach Them
- **Channel:** Google Play search, App Store, Facebook/Instagram ads
- **Message:** "Quick, fun, and light on your phone. Play in seconds."
- **Hook:** Small size + instant fun + fair ads

### Verbatim Language
- "Perfect game for when you're bored"
- "Doesn't eat my battery, love it"
- "Wish there were less ads" (always)
- "Simple but addictive"

## Persona 5: ğŸ® Web Game Browser

### Demographics
- **Age:** 14-30
- **Location:** Global, desktop-heavy markets (US, Europe)
- **Device:** Desktop/laptop (school/work computers where you can't install)
- **Income:** Students, office workers

### Behavior
- **Plays when:** Boredom at school/work, procrastination, "just one game" that becomes 30
- **Session length:** 5-30 minutes (escalates when hooked)
- **Discovery:** Poki, CrazyGames, Google search "free online games", Newgrounds
- **Spending:** Very low. Ad-supported model. Might pay for Steam version if really hooked.
- **Loyalty:** Game-specific, not brand-specific. Follows portals, not developers.

### Motivations
- Instant entertainment (zero commitment)
- Avoiding what they're supposed to be doing
- Familiar genres with a twist
- "One more try" loop

### Frustrations
- "Create account to save progress"
- Games that don't work without installing Flash/Unity plugins
- Lag or poor performance
- Pop-up ads that hijack the page

### How to Reach Them
- **Channel:** Poki/CrazyGames algorithm, Google search, word of mouth at school
- **Message:** "Jump in. No account, no download, no BS."
- **Hook:** Instant play + polished feel + "one more try" loop

### Verbatim Language
- "Can I play this at school?"
- "Finally a browser game that doesn't lag"
- "Bro play this" (with link)
- "I've been playing this for 2 hours I need to stop"
